On Restraint — an excerpt

The page is not a stage. It is a room you invite the reader into.

A typographic system earns its quiet through discipline: a single serif for the body, a single mono for the apparatus, and the smallest possible vocabulary of sizes. The accent — a single mark of terracotta — appears only where attention is genuinely required.

Set in Crimson Pro at a generous measure, the body invites a slower cadence. The mono — IBM Plex — handles the marginalia: dates, captions, section labels, and the small certainties the eye needs to navigate. Together they form a pact: the serif speaks, the mono points.

"Typography is the craft of endowing human language with a durable visual form."

— Robert Bringhurst
